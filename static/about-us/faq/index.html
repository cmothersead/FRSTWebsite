• How good must a swimmer be to join FRST?
    It depends on the swimmer's age:
    • 10 or younger: Must be able to swim 50 yards unassisted freestyle
    • 11-14 years old: Must be able to complete several lengths of the pool without stopping.
    • 15 or older: Must be capable of swimming freestyle for at least 200yards/meters non-stop and familiar with basic concepts of the sport
  • Does my athlete need to be a student within Franklin Community Schools?
    No. FRST does not discriminate based on school/disctrict affiliation, or location of residence. FRST regularly serves athletes from Bloomington, Columbus, various parts of Johnson County and even around the metro Indianapolis area.
  • When can my athlete join FRST?
    Athletes are able to join FRST any time throughout the year. The primary "season breaks" are at the end of March, and end of July at which point we host a dedicated evaluation night for new families, but evaluations can be scheduled at any time. Email daily@frstswimming.com (10&Under) or purcell@frstswimming.com (11-14) to set up an evaluation.
  • What are the costs involved with FRST?
    Costs depend on the training group of the athlete. As athletes progress and move to groups with more practices, membership dues increase, but price per session decreases drastically. We regularly host swim meets at the FCHS pool in order to help subsidize our costs and keep our dues as low as possible!
  • What if an athlete participates in other sports or extra-curriculars?
    FRST takes great pride in offering a "program for everyone" and we understand the need for well-rounded individuals. Each athlete has the opportunity to attend a multitude of offered practices throughout the week and can participate in swimming alongside their other endeavors.
  • Are there required suits/caps/apparel?
    • Suits: FRST offers a team suit each season, available in the Team Store (through Elsmore Swim Shop).
    • Caps: FRST caps are REQUIRED for all COMPETITIONS. Personalized silicon caps are available for purchase at the beginning of each season.
    • Apparel: Each season, included in registration are two team T-shirts which are used as team apparel for competition. Other FRST gear is available via the Team Store.
  • Where does FRST practice?
    FRST practices primarily out of two facilities:
    • Franklin Commuity High School 8-lane, 50 meter pool
    • Franklin Community Middle School 6-lane 25 yard pool
    Occasionally we will utilize other facilities, such as
    • Franklin Family Aquatic Center 6-lane, 50 meter outdoor pool
  • When & How does one swim in meets?
    FRST believes strongly in the importance of meets as the bedrock of swimming. Not only are they a metric of progress, they are a fun way to spend time with teammates, meet new people, and learn important lessons that go beyond the pool! FRST requires home meets for all levels of the competitive team, with a season of leniency for those new to the team. Take a look at our Meet Info page for more on which meets are appropriate, how to sign up, and anything else you might need to know.
  • How are training groups decided?
    Many things are considered by coaches in placing athletes, such as meet performance, practice attendance, stroke mechanics, attitude, etc.
    However, for a simple reference
    • Introductory (Spark & Discovery): Water-safe & comfortable swimming multiple lengths of the pool
    • Developmental (Ignition & Endeavour): Competitively legal in multiple strokes and approaching championship (Divisional) qualification standards
    • Competitive (Launch & Challenger): Qualified for Divisionals in multiple events and committed to the sport (attendance requirements)
    • High-Performance (Exploration & Eclipse): Qualified for State or beyond and highly committed to the sport
