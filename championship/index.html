Olympic Trials

Olympic Trials

Olympic Trials is widely considered the pinnacle of meet qualifications within USA Swimming. The meet occurs once ever four years and is used to select the US Olympic team for that year's games. Qualifying for this event means that the swimmer is one of the top (approximately) 100 swimmers in their respective event in the nation. The top 2 swimmers in each event at the meet are selected to attend the Olympic Games as a part of Team USA.

Nationals

Nationals

USA Swimming Nationals are held each year during the summer. The timing and specifics of this meet are often subject to change based on the schedule of International competitions for the year. (Olympics, World Championships, Pan-Pacific Championships, etc.) On non-Olympic years this meet is used to select the top few swimmers in each event for international competition.

In addition to the Summer National Championships described, most years USA Swimming also hosts a Winter National Championship, which is a slightly less prestigious version of the meet. Some years the meet is hosted in SCY format, and other years in LCM. Its qualification standards are slightly less stringent than Summer Nationals, but the meet is still a showcase of the the top athletes in the nation, both professional and amateur.

Junior Nationals

Junior Nationals

Junior Nationals is the upper echelon of competition for 18&Under athletes in the country. Qualification for this meet is considered very prestigious, and is often the baseline for athletes interested in competing at a high-level NCAA Division 1 university.

Like Nationals, Juniors hosts two iterations of the meet per year:

  • Summer Juniors, held in LCM at the beginning of August
  • Winter Juniors, held in SCY in the middle of December

Also like Nationals, the winter version of the meet accepts more athletes, and is slightly easier to qualify for than its summer counterpart.

Futures

Futures

Futures was created by USA Swimming in 2017 as a stepping stone for athletes to break onto the National/Junior National scene. It is held each year at the beginning of August, alongside Junior and Senior Nationals. In order to qualify for this meet, athletes can be any age, must be faster than the qualification standard for each event, but must be no faster than the Senior National qualifying time for that event.

At roughly the same qualifying standards as this meet are several other meets throughout the year, such as the NCSA Junior National Championships , and the IHSAA State Championships.

Sectionals

Sectionals

Speedo Sectionals is the lowest tier of USA Swimmings national competition offerings. Held in March after short course championships, this meet is an opportunity for those qualified to test their long course skills at the conclusion of the SCY season.

The qualification standards for the meet are just slightly faster than Indiana Swimming's Senior State cuts, so athletes that are qualified for state are often qualified to attend this meet as well.

Zones (14 & Under)

Zones

The Central Zone 14 & Under Championships is a meet held at the beginning of August each year for those swimmers who have achieved AAA times or faster. This meet functions differently than most every other meet because athletes do not represent their home clubs, but instead compete for Team Indiana against the teams fielded by the other LSC's in the Central Zone.

State

State

Indiana Swimming's state championships are a big step for each athlete as they progress in their competitive careers. This level has two different versions of the meet:

  • Age Group State, the 14 & Under version of the meet where athletes compete with their age group (10&Under, 11-12, 13-14)
  • Senior State, a version of the meet open to athletes of all ages, geared toward high-school age athletes

Both of these meets are hosted at the end of each season, March for SCY and July for LCM.

Divisionals

Divisionals

This meet is every swimmer's first exposure to championship-style competition. Swimmers begin by learning that there are meets that you must be fast enough to qualify for, and strive to get their first Divisional cut.

This meet is always held held in a SCY pool, a week before the State Championships begin in March, and a week after they conclude in July.
